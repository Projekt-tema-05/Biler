Citroen

Citroën is a French automobile manufacturer known for its bold engineering and distinctive design philosophy. Founded in 1919 by industrialist André Citroën, the company quickly became a pioneer in automotive innovation, introducing breakthroughs such as Europe’s first mass-produced front-wheel-drive car, the Traction Avant, and the hydropneumatic suspension system featured in the iconic DS. Over the decades, Citroën has built a reputation for comfort, creativity, and unconventional styling, often pushing boundaries within the industry. Today, as part of the Stellantis group, the brand continues to focus on accessible mobility, electric vehicle development, and maintaining its long tradition of quirky yet practical automotive design.
